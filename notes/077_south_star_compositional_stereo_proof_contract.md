# South Star Compositional Stereo Proof Contract

Branch: `south-star`

Date: 2026-05-22

Task: `South Star 214: Define compositional stereo proof contract`

## Purpose

Define the proof contract for the next South Star family:
fixed-molecule compositional stereo scaling.

This contract is deliberately written before adding fixtures. The goal is to
prevent large expected-support files from becoming the authority and to keep
the South Star proof source as molecule facts, typed obligations, traversal
events, solver assignments, rendering, semantic parse-back, and deduplication.

## Scope

This family covers ordinary fixed-molecule stereo composition. It does not
cover:

- query or unspecified bonds;
- dative or coordination bonds;
- aromatic directional overlay policy;
- RDKit writer-string parity;
- parser equivalence as a replacement for exact South Star support generation.

## Authority Names

Use two authority names, depending on the mathematical shape of the case.

### Independent Product Authority

`unified_reference_compositional_stereo_product`

This authority means:

- the molecule decomposes into two or more independent South Star constraint
  components;
- each component has an existing or explicitly named local proof authority;
- the combined support is generated by the product of component assignments
  over shared traversal/rendering skeletons;
- semantic parse-back validates every rendered string;
- fixture strings are a checked artifact, not the source of truth.

Examples that may belong here after inventory:

- two independent acyclic tetrahedral centers;
- disconnected fragment products;
- ring/tetrahedral center plus independent acyclic tetrahedral center;
- independent directional and tetrahedral components.

### Coupled Component Authority

`unified_reference_compositional_stereo_coupled_component`

This authority means:

- two or more stereo obligations share a constraint component;
- the support cannot be justified as a direct product of independent local
  assignments;
- the proof must expose the coupling relation explicitly before any expected
  strings are pinned.

Examples that may belong here after inventory:

- symmetry-coupled tetrahedral centers;
- adjacent stereo centers whose rendered ligand order shares traversal events;
- ring/tetrahedral and directional obligations that share a carrier or closure
  event.

## Required Proof Stages

Every fixture promoted under either authority must have all of these stages:

1. Molecule facts: graph topology, atom facts, bond facts, stereo facts, and
   fragment/ring decomposition.
2. Local obligations: typed tetrahedral, directional, ring-closure, atom-text,
   or bond-text obligations with stable ids.
3. Component partition: an explicit partition of obligations into independent
   or coupled constraint components.
4. Traversal and rendering skeletons: graph traversal, fragment order, ring
   closure events, atom event order, and renderer inputs.
5. Assignment model: local assignment sets for each component and the product
   or coupled solution set for the whole molecule.
6. Rendering: output strings rendered from skeletons and assignments, not
   copied from fixture expectations.
7. Deduplication: first-occurrence deduplication consistent with the South Star
   policy.
8. Semantic evidence: grammar conformance, RDKit parseability, graph signature
   equality, and stereo semantic signature equality.

## Independence Standard

An independent-product proof must show all of the following:

- obligations in different components do not share renderer inputs that affect
  constrained token choice;
- a component assignment can be changed without changing another component's
  valid assignment set;
- total assignment count is the product of component assignment counts for each
  traversal skeleton;
- deduplicated output equality is checked after rendering, not assumed from the
  product count.

If any of these fail, the case belongs under the coupled-component authority or
must remain unpromoted.

## Coupling Standard

A coupled-component proof must name the coupling directly. Acceptable coupling
sources include:

- shared traversal event;
- shared ring-closure event;
- shared directional carrier;
- symmetry relation between ligand-order observations;
- parser-visible constraint that changes semantic identity.

The proof must not encode coupling as a hand-written rejection list of output
strings.

## Minimal Fixture Admission Rule

Do not add a compositional fixture until the witness inventory classifies it as
one of:

- independent product, with a component partition and product-count evidence;
- coupled component, with a named coupling relation and coupled-solver evidence.

Each first fixture should have a small enough support set to review, but the
primary size constraint is proof clarity, not molecule size.

## Manifest Timing

Do not register these authority names in `tests/helpers/south_star_domain_manifest.py`
until the first fixture using the authority is added.

The note is the contract. The manifest is the active checked data surface.
